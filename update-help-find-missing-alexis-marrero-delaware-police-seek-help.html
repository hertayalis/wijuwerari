In early October, police in Delaware began hunting for a missing youngster from Delaware who had been reported missing after disappearing from a busy mall in Maryland.


According to the Maryland State Police, 15-year-old Alexis “Lexi” Marrero went missing from Christiania Mall on the evening of October 1.

Alexis’s phone was located in the mall’s parking lot, and her social media accounts had been dormant ever since she vanished, police said.

On October 2, it is thought that Alexis stayed at a home in Middletown, Delaware, which is around 20 miles south of Christiania Mall.

According to a state police pamphlet, Alexis has been using other people’s phones to hitchhike and locate potential new lodgings.

According to Sergeant India Sturgis’s investigation, “from interviews and other investigator leads we know she contacted multiple individuals of the public to seek assistance in transportation using smartphones and perhaps finding someplace to stay.”

The authorities say she’s 4 feet, 11 inches tall and has brown hair and blue eyes.

Alexis’s mother made a tearful plea to her daughter during a press appearance with Delaware State Police on Wednesday.

Whenever Alexis is in the room, Jennifer Marrero will say, “No one loves you more than me,” and today is no different.

Four days after she went missing, Alexis was reportedly seen in North East, Maryland.

Located 25 miles to the northwest of Middletown and 20 miles to the southwest of Christiania Mall, North East, Maryland is in a very convenient location. Alexis is gone from the Delmarva area, and the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children has issued a missing person alert, noting that she “may be in need of medical assistance.”

Jennifer Marrero stated, “Whatever you’re going through, we’re here to support you, and we shouldn’t be spending the holidays without you.” If you’re with someone who won’t allow you come home, please phone us; we have faith in your intelligence and believe you’ll find a way to get back to us.

Alexis’s parents and siblings claim she’s thriving academically as a freshman at Middletown’s MOT Charter School. She hails from a big, loving family and rides horses regularly.

It is imperative that the Delaware State Police be contacted immediately by anyone with information on Alexis’s whereabouts.
