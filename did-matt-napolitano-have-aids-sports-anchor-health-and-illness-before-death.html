In a recent tragic piece of news, Fox reporter Matt Napolitano passed away with people left wondering the cause of death, with some questioning if he had AIDS. These are only baseless rumors, and the official statement at the moment is that he died due to an auto-immune infection.

As one of the most recognized faces on Fox News, Matt’s personal and professional life had always been of public interest.

Fans recognized him as a funny, witty, and talented reporter and are mourning his untimely demise.

Matt Napolitano was born in 1990 in Pennsylvania, United States, to parents Micheal and Nancy. Although out of the public limelight, the parents instilled key values in Matt early on.

His untimely demise in December 2023 has come as a big shock to fans worldwide. Tributes for the Pennsylvania native are pouring throughout social media.

Did Matt Napolitano Have AIDS?

Fans are perplexed at the cause of Matt’s early passing, with some even wondering if he had AIDS.

We can report that there has been no such official information, and these are just baseless rumors.

Additionally, his partner Ricky stated the cause of death was an autoimmune infection, which Matt had been suffering from for the past 20 years.

In an emotional message on X, Ricky confirmed the death of his partner.

“With a heavy heart, I have to share that my husband passed away yesterday morning. He loved his job and he loved bringing the news to your radios and televisions. An honest journalist who truly cared about facts.”

Regarding his career, Matt joined Fox News in 2015 and swiftly progressed to assume the role of a sports reporter for Fox’s audio platforms. Before that, he worked briefly at TMZ and Merlin Media.

The witty reporter used to be a regular on “Cavuto: Coast to Coast,” covering sports stories. He also worked as an anchor and reporter at various times.

In 2022, he took a break from Fox News but returned full-time later that year when Fox decided to focus more on audio stuff.

An alumnus of Hofstra University, Matt was loved by his peers for his charming and funny personality, nicknaming him “Matty Naps.” Additionally, Matt has two siblings, brother Micheal and sister Carole.

He was also an avid fan of TV and reality shows in his formative years. This is evidenced by his appearances on “Jeopardy” and “Wheel of Fortune” as a contestant on the small screen.

In terms of his personal life, he was an openly gay reporter married to Ricky Whitcomb. The two shared a loving and caring relationship.

The pair exchanged wedding vows in May 2023. His untimely death will certainly leave a hole in Ricky and other family member’s lives.

Matt Napolitano Tribute

It is clear that people in the media sector, fans, and peers loved Matt infinitely. There have been tributes, emotional messages, and appreciation posts all over social media for the late reporter.

“Matt did everything from anchoring to writing and producing for our audio platforms and loved being able to work in the journalism field that he trained for his entire life,” stated Fox News CEO Suzanne Scott.

People will certainly miss the charm and vibe Matt brought to any presentation. The void his death leaves behind for the news and media world will be difficult to fill.
